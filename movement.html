Definition

Utilizing the Flow of lines to guide the eye's movement through a picture or page, and they often have different meanings based on the type of line. Vertical lines will convey left to right movement, horizontal lines will convey up and down movement, and curving lines are more meandering (Hagen and Golombisky 58).

In Summary

Thinking about in what order you want things to be seen on a page, it's best to create a flow using the assets of the page to best guide what they see when. This is easy to do using different lines to guide where items go and where the eye is guided.

Real World Context

Use movement to guide the layout or picture. Decide whether a vertical, horizontal, or maybe even meandering line will give the vibe you want. Motion blur isn't always bad.
