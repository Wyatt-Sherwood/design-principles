Definition

The perception that items which are close together are in the same group (Hagen and Golombisky 60).

In Summary

When you put things together, there is an assumption that those things are in the same group. If you have one pile of Skittles, and then split it in half it is now seen as two piles of Skittles rather than the same bag you just poured out.

Real World Context

Keep this in mind in how you want your content to be organized. If you want people to know that two paragraphs are separate and relate to different things, then separate them into different blocks so the consumer can visually see that they are separate.
