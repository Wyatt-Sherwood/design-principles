Definition

Mentally filling the gaps of a figure to form a more common, perceived shape (Hagen and Golombisky 62).

In Summary

When we see a figure that has negative space, our minds will fill the gap of the negative space to create a more understandable shape. You take 4 pencils and align them into a square, but don't actually have the corners come in contact. Your brain won't see 4 pencils sitting in paired parallels, your brain will see the square.

Real World Context

Using incomplete shapes if a good way to keep a page interesting and to save on space. Rather than showing the entire shape, use the closure theory to your advantage and use an incomplete form. This is especially useful in logo design.
